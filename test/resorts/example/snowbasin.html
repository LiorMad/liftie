    • Mid Mountain

      37°F

      • Partly Cloudy

      • Wind 4 mph NE

The weather continues to look promising as we close in on the weekend, with models suggesting we may finally have some snow headed our way. Today will be partly sunny with a high near 39° and increasing cloud coverage through the day as a storm moves in this evening. We’ve been waiting for Mother Nature to bring us more snow, and we hope our patience pays off. This first storm between Thursday and Friday looks somewhat weaker, but the possibility of a stronger one Saturday to Sunday remains, so keep those snow dances going! 

Until then, operations remain similar today, with five lifts spinning and 17 open runs, with 11 covered in fresh corduroy overnight. The groomers are still firing, giving you access to top-notch top-to-bottom skiing to prepare for the rest of the season. Snowmaking is still in full effect, so the combination of snowmaking efforts and incoming storms should help us open more terrain over the next couple of weeks.

Please remember that you’ll find early-season, low-tide hazards throughout the resort. We can’t wait for sections to fill in, but until then look out for both marked and unmarked obstacles. Slow down, give everyone their space, and let’s make it a great start to the season so we can all enjoy the rest of the year. 

Happy Thursday!

Access Gates Safety Information Toggle accordion

All guests must use access gates regardless of website status. Website information is intended to inform the guest of terrain status, but it is the guest’s responsibility to confirm the area is open by entering through an access gate.  Signage on the mountain supersedes website information. Failure to use access gates may result in loss of pass privileges.
